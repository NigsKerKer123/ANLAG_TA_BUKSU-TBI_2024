IV. Definition of Terms

To ensure a clear and common understanding of the terms used in this Manual of Operations, the following definitions are provided:

  • Angel Investor. An individual who provides capital for startups, usually in exchange for ownership equity or convertible debt. Angel investors are often critical for early-stage funding.
  • Business Incubation. A structured support process designed to nurture startups from the idea stage to full-fledged businesses through access to infrastructure, mentorship, funding, and business services.
  • Business Model. The framework a startup uses to generate revenue and deliver value to customers. It answers questions like what the business will offer (products/services), who the target customers are, and how the business will make money.
  • Business Plan. A detailed document that outlines a startup’s business model, market strategy, financial projections, and operational plan. It serves as a roadmap for how the startup will grow and achieve profitability.
  • Incubation Agreement. A formal contract between the TBI and a startup, outlining the roles, responsibilities, and terms of engagement for the startup during its time in the incubator.
  • Market Validation. The process of determining whether a product has a potential target market that is willing to pay for it. This step helps startups assess the commercial viability of their ideas.
  • Memorandum of Agreement (MOA). A formal document outlining the terms of a partnership or collaboration between two or more parties. In the context of the TBI, MOAs may be signed between the incubator, startups, and external partners such as government agencies, investors, or industry players.
  • Minimum Viable Product (MVP). A version of a product with just enough features to satisfy early customers and gather feedback for future development. Startups often develop MVPs to test market demand before investing heavily in product development.
  • Startup. A newly established company or venture that is in the early stages of operations. In the context of the TBI, startups typically focus on leveraging technology and innovation to solve real-world problems.
  • Spin-off. A new business created based on the research and development (R&D) activities of an academic institution or a parent organization. A spin-off typically commercializes intellectual property (IP) or technology developed within a university or research center.
  • Technology Business Incubator (TBI). An entity that supports technology-based startups by offering mentorship, resources, and infrastructure to help them grow into viable businesses. The Anlag Ta Buksu TBI serves as Bukidnon State University’s platform for fostering innovation, entrepreneurship, and commercialization of research outputs.
  • Technology Commercialization. The process of bringing new technologies to the marketplace. This can involve transferring research innovations from the university to private enterprises for development into marketable products or services.
  • Unique Value Proposition (UVP). The feature or benefit that makes a product or service stand out from the competition. It is the reason customers would choose the startup’s product over others in the market.
  • Venture Capitalist (VC). A professional investor or investment firm that provides capital to startups with high growth potential in exchange for an equity stake. VCs typically invest larger amounts than angel investors and are more involved in scaling the business.

More about ANLAG TA BUKSU-TBI

  • The Anlag Ta Buksu Technology Business Incubator (TBI) is a unique university-based incubator in Mindanao that supports new ventures by providing essential infrastructure, resources, and business guidance. It offers office and laboratory spaces with advanced technology, access to specialized equipment for prototyping and testing, and mentorship from experienced entrepreneurs and industry professionals. Additionally, it connects startups with investors, funding agencies, and government resources to foster growth. More than just a workspace, the TBI serves as a collaborative ecosystem, linking entrepreneurs with academia, government, and the private sector to promote innovation and development.
